### Question  ###
A 45-year-old never smoking woman with metastatic lung adenocarcinoma has limited EBUS TBNA material. Screening immunohistochemistry for ROS1 is strongly positive. To confirm the alteration and qualify for targeted therapy, which specific assay is most appropriate?
 ### Options### 
(a) ROS1 RNA expression microarray
(b) ROS1 break apart fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH)
(c) Serum ROS1 protein ELISA
(d) DNA methylation panel of the ROS1 promoter
(e) KRAS codon 12/13 PCR
###Discussion###
Correct answer: b

ROS1 break apart FISH (choice b) is an FDA recognized companion diagnostic approach for detecting ROS1 rearrangements and confirming positive IHC screens for therapy eligibility. The FDA companion diagnostic landscape includes ISH/FISH, IHC, PCR, and NGS assays; for some gene fusions (e.g., ROS1), break apart FISH is the validated confirmatory method used for selection of specific TKIs. Reviews of oncogenic fusions in lung cancer similarly highlight ROS1 rearrangements as targetable events typically confirmed by FISH or detected in multiplex fusion panels.
(a) RNA expression arrays are not the standard confirmatory CDx. (c) No validated serum ELISA determines ROS1 fusion status. (d) Promoter methylation is unrelated to detecting gene fusions. (e) KRAS testing does not assess ROS1 rearrangements and may be mutually exclusive with certain driver fusions.
Pearl: Use IHC as a screen but confirm ROS1 with a validated CDx—commonly break apart FISH—when tissue is limited and targeted therapy is contemplated.
